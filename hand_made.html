Essential Tools & Materials

  • Copper clad board (single or double-sided)
  • Ferric chloride or other etching solution
  • Permanent markers or transfer paper
  • Plastic containers for etching
  • Drill with small bits (0.8mm - 1.2mm)
  • Soldering iron and solder
  • Rubber gloves and safety glasses
  • Multimeter for testing
Module 3: Circuit Design Fundamentals
Master the basics of circuit design for handmade PCBs. Learn about component placement, trace width, and design considerations.

Design Considerations

When designing circuits for handmade PCBs, keep these key principles in mind:

  • Simplicity: Keep designs simple and avoid complex routing when possible
  • Spacing: Maintain adequate spacing between traces (at least 1mm)
  • Trace Width: Use wider traces for power lines (at least 1.5mm)
  • Component Placement: Group related components together
Module 4: PCB Etching Techniques
Learn various etching techniques for creating handmade PCBs, including toner transfer, direct drawing, and photosensitive methods.

Etching Methods

Toner Transfer Method Popular
Direct Drawing Method Simple
Step 1

Design Preparation

Create or print your circuit design in mirror image (for toner transfer) or prepare your copper board for direct drawing.

Step 2

Transfer Design

Transfer your design to the copper board using heat (for toner transfer) or draw directly with a permanent marker.

Step 3

Prepare Etching Solution

Mix ferric chloride according to instructions or prepare your etching solution in a plastic container.

Step 4

Etching Process

Immerse the board in the etching solution and agitate gently until all exposed copper is removed.

Module 5: Drilling and Component Mounting
Learn techniques for drilling holes in your PCB and mounting components for soldering. Master the art of precision drilling and component placement.

Drilling Tips

Follow these guidelines for successful drilling:

  • Drill Bit Size: Use 0.8mm for ICs and small components, 1.0mm for standard components
  • Drill Speed: Use a high-speed drill at moderate speed to avoid damaging the board
  • Center Punch: Mark hole centers with a center punch to prevent drill bits from slipping
  • Support: Place the board on a wooden surface while drilling
Module 6: Soldering and Testing
Master the art of soldering components to your handmade PCB and learn how to test your finished circuit for functionality and troubleshoot common issues.

Soldering Techniques

Testing Your Circuit

Follow these steps to test your handmade PCB:

  • Visual Inspection: Check for solder bridges, cold joints, or missing connections
  • Continuity Test: Use a multimeter to check connections between components
  • Power Test: Apply power gradually and check for heating components
  • Functional Test: Test each function of your circuit
  • Troubleshooting: Systematically identify and fix problems
